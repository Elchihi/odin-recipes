Egyptian Molokhia

Description

Molokhia is a type of leafy plant called Corchorus, Jews mallow, Jute mallow or Nalta. It is said that Molokhia started from Egypt and then spread to the rest of the Middle East, North Africa and East Mediterranean regions. It can be found in different forms from fresh leaves, dried or frozen variety.

ingredients

  • The molokhia leaves or frozen molokhia
  • beef or chicken stock
  • fried garlic & coriander to make tasha
  • butter
  • salt

Steps

  1. In a deep pot over medium-high heat melt butter
  2. Saut√© garlic for 15-30 seconds Until lightly golden and fragrant
  3. Add coriander and keep stirring for another 30 seconds
  4. The coriander will give its fragrance and turn a little more dark brown
  5. Pour in some of the broth keeping the rest after we check consistency at the end
  6. Sprinkle the bouillon cube. Stir until it is dissolved
  7. Add the Molokhia, and turn down the heat to medium low, keep stirring every now and then
  8. Molokhia will slowly melt in the broth. Wait until it has completely melted
  9. Check the seasoning and sweetness: if you need salt
  10. Serve with rice and Aish Baladi
