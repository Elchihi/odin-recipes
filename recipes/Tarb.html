Egyptian Tarb

Description

Tarb is a traditional Egyptian dish made from minced meat, typically lamb or beef, that is wrapped in caul fat(a thin, lacy membrane of fat surrounding the internal organs of animals). The minced meat is usually mixed with spices, herbs, and sometimes finely chopped onions or garlic to enhance the flavor.

ingredients

  • One medium onion
  • 1 kg minced lamb
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon spices
  • 1 tablespoon parsley
  • Tarb (lamb fat)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water

Steps

  1. Chop the onion and add to the minced lamb. Then add the salt, pepper, spices and parsley. Mix well together and leave for at least one hour
  2. Mold the kofta in finger like shapes, and cut the Tarb into small pieces. Place the kofta fingers inside the tarb pieces and roll each one separately
  3. Stack the fingers on a baking tray and season with salt and pepper. Add water
  4. lace the baking tray in an oven on medium heat for one and a half hours until cooked. Serve
